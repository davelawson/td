# Product State: td

`PRODUCT.md` is the durable source of truth for what `td` does for users right now. Update it whenever the repository gains, removes, or materially changes a user-visible workflow, capability, limitation, or scope boundary.

## Product Summary

`td` is planned as a PC tower-defense game prototype for a single local player. The intended game blends exploration, base-building, resource gathering, and conventional tower-defense encounters in a medieval wizardry fantasy setting.

The current repository ships a small playable shell that starts in Management on a grasslands home Plot containing sparse Tree, Boulder, and Iron Deposit terrain, the Sanctum as its only initial structure, 100 Wood, 50 Stone, 20 Iron, 0 Gold, and zero inhabitants. Peaceful play consists of an instantaneous Labour phase followed by an open-ended Management phase. Labour begins after each successful Raid, resolves every placed economic building once, and immediately enters Management; Management lasts until the player clicks `Next Raid`. During Management, magnifying-glass buttons appear on borders between explored and unexplored orthogonal Plots. Each available destination receives a permanent 50/50 grasslands-or-hills assignment when it first becomes adjacent to explored land, and that biome name appears beside its button before terrain is revealed. Clicking the circular button reveals the connected Plot for free; the label is informational. Home and explored grasslands generate 6% Tree, 3% Boulder, and 1% Iron Deposit, leaving 90% empty grass; hills generates 3% Tree, 6% Boulder, and 3% Iron Deposit, leaving 88% empty grass. Trees, Boulders, and Iron Deposits can be selected to show terrain and biome details with a gold Tile outline; empty grass and Road cannot be selected. They block construction and are exhaustible inputs for economic buildings. A Woodcutter, Stone Quarry, or Iron Mine consumes the nearest matching Tree, Boulder, or Iron Deposit anywhere in the explored Domain before adding 10 Wood, Stone, or Iron during Labour; no matching terrain means no payout. Consumed grasslands and hills terrain becomes grass. The home north road, north-chain roads, and shared explored edges overwrite generated obstacles so routes and Plot joins remain clear. Adjacent explored Plots render without plot frames or padding between them, and northward exploration extends the visible center road and the deterministic Raid spawn point. The map camera supports mouse-wheel zoom, `WASD` panning, and right-drag panning from the game view, with visible screen-space UI blocking drag starts. The 260-pixel building bar appears only during Management, groups build choices into `Housing`, `Economic`, and `Defenses` tabs, defaults to `Housing`, and shows each building's cost and population or staffing values to the right of each icon. Hovering a building icon opens an informational tooltip with its description, cost, staffing requirement, and implemented effect or combat stats. House costs 20 Wood, requires no staff, and immediately grants 2 available and total Peasants when placed. Barracks costs 10 Wood and 10 Stone, consumes 2 available and total Peasants, and grants 2 available and total Soldiers. Dorm costs 10 Wood and 10 Stone, consumes 1 available and total Peasant, and grants 1 available and total Apprentice. Woodcutter, Stone Quarry, and Iron Mine each reserve one available Peasant. Market costs 30 Wood and reserves two available Peasants plus one available Soldier. Tower construction requires both resources and available staff. A successful staffed build reserves staff by reducing available counts while preserving totals. Buildable icon squares use green outlines; buildings without enough resources, population, or staff use red outlines and draw their icons at 70% opacity. Selection, deterministic scaling Raids, projectile combat, rewards, pause, overlay, and menu behavior remain implemented. The centered top bar previews the next challenge during Management and shows the frozen generated challenge during Raid and breach. A Raid's challenge comes from its number, total settlement population, and all explored Plots. Over a `5 + 2 * sqrt(challenge)` second window, Skeletons, Zombies, Ghouls, and Armoured Skeletons enter at progress-scaled score multiples of 2, 4, 6, and 8, so later rosters spawn increasingly quickly while the first Raid remains nine seconds long with two Skeletons and one Zombie. Tower-damage defeats award only Gold: Skeletons drop 1, Zombies 2, Ghouls 3, and Armoured Skeletons 5. Barricade removal and breach award nothing. Distinct raider types differ in speed by at least three percent so simultaneous spawns do not remain perfectly overlapped. Both new enemy types deal 1 Sanctum damage.

## Users and Jobs To Be Done

The current user is the developer-player validating whether the game can become a playable local prototype. Their immediate job is to run the desktop application, confirm that a visible game window appears, navigate the small menu flow, and confirm that the quit path closes cleanly.

Future players are expected to want a strategy game where they explore, build a base, gather resources, and defend against threats with tower-defense mechanics. Only the first limited tower placement action exists today; the broader versions of those systems belong to the roadmap rather than current product truth.

## Current Capabilities

### Project Direction

`Core`: The repository records the product name, intended genre, target platform phase, art direction, runtime stack, and first implementation slice. This gives contributors enough context to plan work without relying on chat history.

### Agent Runtime Defaults

`Core`: `.codex/config.toml` keeps Codex project defaults for trusted local work. These settings are agent configuration only and are not application runtime configuration.

### Planning Workflow

`Core`: Substantial work must use an ordered ExecPlan under `plans/`, following `PLANS.md`. `plans/00-initial-ebitengine-menu.md` initialized the Go module and Ebitengine app. `plans/01-expanded-main-menu.md` expanded the main menu flow. `plans/03-new-game-configuration.md` adds the Wizard name configuration screen. `plans/04-resolution-and-pixel-text-scaling.md` sets the 1920x1080 default window and current resize policy. `plans/05-main-game-update-loop.md` adds the first game state, update counter, and pause behavior. `plans/06-ingame-menu-overlay.md` adds the ESC in-game overlay menu. `plans/10-basic-camera.md` adds mouse-wheel zoom and `WASD` camera panning for the game scene. `plans/40-right-drag-camera.md` adds right-drag camera panning from the game view.

### Runtime Shell

`Core`: The repository has a Go module, an Ebitengine executable under `cmd/td/`, typed runtime assets, menu and game packages, a Sanctum-only starting Plot, status state, camera zoom plus keyboard and right-drag panning, structure, raider, Tree, Boulder, and Iron Deposit selection behavior, a Management-only widened tabbed building bar with right-side construction costs, population-grant metadata, staffing metadata, informational hover tooltips, green/red capacity outlines, capacity opacity feedback, drag placement, live and frozen challenge display, deterministic scaling Raids, and Bow, Flame Bolt, and Catapult projectile combat. Tests cover these behaviors without depending on free starting towers.

### Missing Gameplay And Operations

`Core`: There is currently no tower upgrade, exploration cost, scouting-versus-claiming distinction, manual resource gathering, terrain regrowth, timed inhabitant recruitment, reassignment, staff release, save system, or campaign system. House, Barracks, Dorm, economic-building, and tower placement are Management actions gated by their resources, population costs, and staffing requirements. Successful Raids advance the Day and immediately resolve terrain-consuming Labour, which is the only implemented resource production; a breached Raid performs no Labour. Population totals can increase through Houses, Barracks Soldier grants, and Dorm Apprentice grants; Peasant totals can decrease through Barracks and Dorm conversion; and available counts can decrease through economic buildings and tower construction.

## Core Workflows

### Repository Bootstrap

A contributor opens the repository, reads the root control documents, and sees that `td` is a local Go/Ebitengine PC game prototype. The bootstrap workflow has already produced project-specific control documents and the first runnable menu shell.

### Main Menu Workflow

A contributor runs `go run ./cmd/td`, starts a new game, and sees `Management phase | Challenge 4.0` above a tower-free grasslands home Plot with sparse Trees, Boulders, and rare Iron Deposits, the Sanctum as its only initial structure, and a clear road north. The home Plot uses the same 6% Tree, 3% Boulder, 1% Iron Deposit, and 90% empty-grass weights as explored grasslands, without an additional guaranteed clearing around the Sanctum. Clicking a Tree, Boulder, or Iron Deposit selects it with a gold Tile outline and opens a panel naming its terrain and Plot biome; clicking empty grass or Road clears selection. Magnifying-glass buttons sit on borders to unexplored north, east, south, and west Plots. Each has a stable `Grasslands` or `Hills` label placed on its unexplored side so the contributor can compare destinations before choosing. Clicking only the circular button during Management reveals the connected Plot immediately, and this also works while SPACE-paused. The revealed Plot uses the previewed biome; grasslands is Tree-biased with 1% Iron Deposit, while hills is Boulder-biased with 3% Iron Deposit and 88% empty grass. New labeled choices appear as exploration expands, and shared frontier Plots keep their first assignment. No plot-level frame, padding, or terrain ring separates adjacent explored Plots. Northward revealed Plots also show a straight center road, and the next Raid enters from the farthest explored north road. The player can inspect the map with mouse-wheel zoom, `WASD` panning, or by pressing the right mouse button over the game view and dragging; right-drag starts are ignored over the top bar, visible building bar, Next Raid button, selection panel, and overlay UI. The top bar shows 100 Wood, 50 Stone, 20 Iron, 0 Gold, `0/0` for every inhabitant role, and the upcoming challenge. The left building bar appears only during Management, defaults to the `Housing` tab, and shows values to the right of each icon. Hovering any building icon shows a tooltip to the right of the bar with a short description, cost, staffing, and the building's current production, population effect, or combat stats. The House icon is available immediately with a green outline; dragging it onto an empty grass-like Tile spends 20 Wood and changes Peasants to `2/2`. Barracks and Dorm are shown with red outlines and 70% icon opacity until enough Peasants exist. Switching to `Economic` exposes Woodcutter, Stone Quarry, Iron Mine, and Market. A Woodcutter can then be placed for 10 Wood, reserving one Peasant as `1/2`; after the next successful Raid, Day 2 Labour consumes the nearest Tree in any explored Plot, adds 10 Wood, replaces that Tree with grass, and immediately opens Management. Stone Quarry and Iron Mine follow the same pattern with Boulders for Stone and Iron Deposits for Iron. Economic buildings remain placeable only on empty grass; their matching terrain can be anywhere in the explored Domain. If none remains, the building produces nothing and retries during the next Labour. A Market costs 30 Wood and requires two Peasants plus one Soldier; selecting it during Management, including while SPACE-paused, shows buttons beside the building that buy one Wood or Stone for 1 Gold and one Iron for 3 Gold. The Barracks icon becomes eligible once two Peasants are available; dragging it onto an empty grass-like Tile spends 10 Wood and 10 Stone, changes Peasants to `0/0`, and changes Soldiers to `2/2`. The Dorm icon becomes eligible once one Peasant is available; dragging it onto an empty grass-like Tile spends 10 Wood and 10 Stone, changes Peasants from `2/2` to `1/1` after one House, and changes Apprentices to `1/1`. Switching to `Defenses` exposes Bow, Flame Bolt, and Catapult Towers. Although starting resources cover Bow and Flame Bolt, neither tower icon highlights or starts dragging until required staff exist. Successful staffed placement reduces the relevant available count and leaves its total unchanged. Starting a Raid hides the building bar, releases its former region to map input, and retains the generated challenge beside the remaining-enemy count.

The top bar groups Apprentice, Soldier, and Peasant icons after the resources. Each shows `available/total`; available inhabitants can be reserved by economic buildings or tower construction, while total remains unchanged.

## Product Constraints and Known Limits

- The current target is a local prototype only.
- Distribution, release packaging, CI, license selection, and store targets are deferred.
- The current playable shell includes a Sanctum-only starting Plot and a Management-only tabbed building bar whose resource, population, and staffing requirements jointly gate placement. The same building bar can place Houses, which cost Wood and grant Peasants; Barracks, which cost Wood and Stone and convert Peasants into Soldiers; Dorms, which cost Wood and Stone and convert one Peasant into one Apprentice; and economic buildings, which reserve Peasants and consume matching explored terrain to produce resources during Labour. The bar shows values beside icons, shows informational hover tooltips for icon details, uses green outlines for buildable icon squares, and uses red outlines plus 70% icon opacity for capacity-blocked buildings. Raid and breach hide the bar and do not reserve its screen region.
- Saving the game and campaign structure are explicit non-goals for the first phase.
- Starting a new game opens Day 1 Management with `0/0` inhabitants and free exploration buttons for adjacent Plots. House construction can add Peasants, Barracks construction can convert those Peasants into Soldiers, Dorm construction can convert one Peasant into one Apprentice, and producers can turn reserved Peasants plus exhaustible terrain into Labour-phase Wood, Stone, or Iron, while a staffed Market trades combat-earned Gold for those materials. Staff committed to economic buildings or towers cannot currently be released because structure removal and reassignment are not implemented.
- Settings are represented only by a placeholder screen; no configurable options exist yet.
- Exploration is implemented only as free adjacent Plot reveal during Management. Trees, Boulders, and Iron Deposits are non-buildable terrain consumed by their matching economic buildings from anywhere in the explored Domain; they do not constrain where those buildings are placed. Terrain regrowth, direct gathering, broader scouting, claiming, exploration costs, and broader tower-defense gameplay are not implemented.

## Non-Goals

The current phase is not trying to build campaign progression, save/load, multiplayer, online services, release packaging, or a full tower-defense encounter. The current Raid slice deterministically scales Skeleton, Zombie, Ghoul, and Armoured Skeleton counts and release timing from Raid number, settlement population, and explored Plots, while retaining one fixed north-road path, player-built towers, and tiered Gold rewards and Market trading. The project is also not trying to choose final art production workflows before the game loop proves useful.
